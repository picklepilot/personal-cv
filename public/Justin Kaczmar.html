Why am I a great fit?

Firstly, I have exclusively been using TailwindCS/UI as a front-end framework solution in every project for several years. Your job posting, specifically the "Who you are" section, seemed tailor-made to my being for the most part. It would only make sense to let me explain by expanding on some of the "Who you are" points."

You love to design — in code.

This is my Bat Signal. This is how I live my professional life. Design-in-code is core to my operation. So much could be said about this quality. To be concise, I always had an appreciation for design. It's what led me to becoming a developer by trade. My first full time job was at an agency that is run by designers and architects. It was there that I learned how vital design is to the success of a project. It was at my current job that I really picked up on how valuable being able to speak the language of design as a developer can be.

For example, HAMR is the project that I've been working on for the past 7 years at 3M. I know my way around Figma very well, but for the most recent version of HAMR (v2, freshly/successfully rolled out 🎉), I was able to re-design fully in code—a large nod to y'all for that one 😉.

You’re fanatical about polish.

Can very much thank MBLM for this one as well—at least professionally. I have experience as a "pixel perfectionist" in the extremes of workplace environments, one end being the classic digital design agency and the other being an multinational conglomerate where being extremely lean is vital to survival. (Note: It's very difficult to convince one of the world's largest companies' employees that your thing will be better than the thing they currently use. Jacob's Law ain't a joke.)

Bottom line: Being able to work where "good enough" is permanently replaced by "fanatical about polish" sounds like a place where I can thrive.

You dive deep. && You live on the bleeding edge. && You love to learn new tools.

These three points exist in tandem for me, both in my professional and personal design/development life.

On the professional side, I mentioned before that it's extremely difficult to convince a workforce to transition from their current thing to my more modern and prettier thing. That's why I was brought into 3M 7 years ago and have stayed to this point. I've been able to consistently discover and apply new technologies/optimizations to achieve the unachievable by other software being used.

On the personal side, that's pretty much what bibli is for. It's been an amazing way for me to keep focused on the latest and greatest with respect to my toolkit.

You are a user and champion of the Tailwind Labs products.

Note: I somewhat put these words in your mouths, but I just wanted to drive this point home. 😜

Over the past several years, I have come to rely heavily on Tailwind as my exclusive CSS framework. It would be amazing to be able to wake up every day knowing that I am contributing to something that provides an impact that I've personally felt and can understand.

Here's what excites me personally:

  • ...to be a part of a team that is as passionate about the work as I am.
  • ...to have the means to contribute to larger conversation on bridging gaps between design and development.

  • Introducing more enterprise level components that achieve more complex business UIs
    • Interesting ways of visualizing KPIs
    • Complex query builder
    • Advance the dashboard-eque templates
